About Me

  • Hi! I'm Camila.

I am a third-year Northwestern student pursuing a Mechanical Engineering Major, Latino Studies Minor, and the Segal Design Certificate. I am an outgoing, dedicated dreamer who loves to tinker and design quality products that build community, speak for themselves, and have personality.

I am most proud of my work when it benefits others and solves problems, whether small or big. As a multifaceted designer, I lean into each part of the design process so that my end products reflect a focused vision.

This past summer, I had the opportunity to intern at Caterpillar where I designed spark plug adaptors for internal testing. I am also a Shop Trainer in the Segal Design Shop at Northwestern, where I connect my communication and education to the people and projects that flow in and out of the workspace.

My Story

My Story

  • Going beyond functionality; showing the personality behind the purpose in design.

Every Saturday, my dad would take me with him to work. I'd sit in a not-yet finished home in the Texas heat and stare at stirring cement, a tool box, screw guns, and 2x4's. When I was six, I built a bench. I became an engineer.

Every weekday, my Mom would pick me up from school and sit me in a salon chair. I'd listen in on multilingual conversations and mess with mannequins' hair. I learned to listen. I braided my cousin's hip-length hair in a way that kept her from getting so hot. I became a designer.

Design and engineering are my tools towards meaningful human interaction and understanding. I've learned technique and technology, and I hope to continue to connect.
